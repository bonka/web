(The Questions)

How would you frame this challenge?

Framing determines what's in the shot and what's left out. By reframing business challenges, we can reveal new perspectives and possibilities that weren't visible before. What's currently outside your frame that might change everything if brought into view?

What's your next scene?

If nothing changes, it's just exposition. Every scene in a good story represents a turning point. What's the next meaningful change in your organization's journey? Who are the protagonists, and what obstacles must they overcome?

Where are you in your story?

Understanding your place in the narrative arc helps determine what comes next. Are you in the setup, confronting the main challenge, or approaching resolution? Knowing where you are helps chart the path forward.

What should be in sharp focus?

In cinematography, focus directs attention. In business, focus determines priorities. What deserves to be in sharp focus right now, and what can remain in the background? How might pulling focus to different elements change your understanding?

(About Me)

With over 15 years of experience spanning business strategy, creative direction, and filmmaking, I bring a unique perspective to organizational challenges. As Director of Innovation at Sopra Steria, I've led strategic innovation initiatives that help leaders gain knowledge and confidence to understand and be understood.

My background in filmmaking—directing a TV series, creating a documentary about 9/11, and writing/directing an award-winning short film—informs my approach to business transformation. I see business challenges through the lens of storytelling, visual composition, and human connection.

I believe that "Companies don't change companies. People do." My work focuses on the human dimension of transformation, helping organizations navigate complexity by creating clarity, building shared understanding, and revealing new possibilities.
